Standardisation Methods

Standardization is critical in analytical chemistry to ensure accurate and reproducible results. These are the three fundamental pillars.

Primary Standards

Primary standards are highly pure, stable chemical substances used as the ultimate reference point. They must have a high molar mass, be non-hygroscopic, and be available in high purity (e.g., >99.9%).

Examples: Potassium Hydrogen Phthalate (KHP), Sodium Carbonate.

Secondary Standards

These refer to solutions whose concentration has been determined by titration against a primary standard. They are used for routine analysis but require periodic re-standardization.

Examples: Hydrochloric Acid (HCl), Sodium Hydroxide (NaOH) solutions.

üíß

Titrimetric Standardization

The process of determining the exact concentration of a solution by reacting it with a standard solution. This involves careful addition of titrant until the equivalence point is reached.

Key Concept: Accuracy depends on precise volume measurement and indicator selection.
